Photo Gift World [PGW]




Photo Gift World [Closed] was a manufacture that produced custom photo gifts




Description

For over 10 years I worked at PGW developing the software that managed the many aspects of the gifting process, all the way thru to the customer.


Key features

  • Automated printing of 1000's of orders per day to numerous printers
  • Itemsdue were monitors placed thru-out the facility listing the states of orders per department
  • Barcode tracking of orders thru each stage of production
  • 500+ retail location shipping with packing slips, and ship to home orders across Canada and US
  • Customer Service web portal allowing retailers to login and view thier orders and status
  • Full Waybill tracking and monitoring for delivery and in-store closing of waybills
  • In-Store automated printing and manufactuing in a mini-lab

Automated Printing

  • 10,000's of orders were delivered daily, and using a custom plugin for CorelDraw they were automatically sent to print
  • Prints to Inkjet printers for Mugs, Calendars were printed on Large Xerox printer, canvas prints, laser engravers...
  • Photos were imported into a template and could include seasonal graphic designs
  • Peak time was during Chrismas with 18,000 orders in one day ove 120gig at 160,000 photo's
  • The entire systems was data driven, orders were processed into a database and sent to print in-order of recieved

Itemsdue

  • Do to the shear volume of orders, being able to internally identify any orders not moving was key
  • Orders were split into 13 different departments and each had their own monitor and order list
  • Orders were color coded on screen if their were issues, and grayscale coded if they hadn't seen any movement at all
  • They were all seperated by process either in printing, production, in shipping, and dropped off when shipped
  • A seperate system was built to monitor the bulk shippmemts being sent to the retailers

Customer Support Site

  • We learned early on that transparency was key, providing a web portal to track the retailer own orders
  • Full tracking thru the entire process from manufacturing, shipping to who signed for package at the store
  • A single location may of only had a couple hundered orders but we processed them all so barcoding tracking made it easier to identify issues
  • Once we add this and trained the stores our call volume dropped nearly 90%
  • We eventually added a customer tracking portal as well that allowed customer to monitor their own orders as well

Retail - 1hr Photo gifts

  • We had a call to action from one of our major retails to bring value to their own labs
  • Most retails had only done in-house photo printing and wanted to make small gift item in-store in 1hr
  • I was able to refactor our CorelDraw plugin to communicate via an internal API and pull down thier own select orders.
  • We installed a basic computer and printer in 400+ retail locations, and automatically printed order ready to make in minutes
  • We topped out at 18,000 mugs in 1 day, 400 locations, 3 days before Chrismas all made within 1-2 hours of dropping while customers shopped
  • This had the added benifit of pre-shipping our heaviest item a 1/2 lbs ceramic mug to the stores, saving on weight and break-age

Shipping - Web Scraping

  • 2007 we had issue where DHL said they had delivered a shippment when in reality they didn't
  • At the time they didnt have an API so i built a web scrapper that once an hour would pull all our waybills
  • From that we discovered nearly 65% of our shippments were 2-3 days late, some even 5+ days late
  • We moved to UPS the following year, where for the next 4 years we were the #2 shipping in Canada nearly 4 tons a day
  • We learned that year that A-Z was the only way to operate, verify everything, trust that anyhting in the chain can or will break
